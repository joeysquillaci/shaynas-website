About Me

Purpose-Driven, Hands-On, and Community-Focused

I am a medical student who values thoughtful patient care, meaningful service, and collaborative learning. My path in medicine has been shaped by work with underserved communities, hands-on clinical training, and a deep interest in surgical care and women’s health equity.

What Matters to Me

Advocacy and Equity

I am especially passionate about improving access to breast cancer surgery and reconstruction for women in low-resource settings. I hope to contribute to care models that reduce barriers and improve long-term outcomes.

Hands-On Medicine

From anatomy prosection and free-clinic work to surgical volunteer experiences, I feel most energized when combining technical precision with compassionate, patient-centered care.
